HiNAS Control

Avikus, a subsidiary of HD Hyundai, is a leading innovator in AI-driven autonomous navigation. Its flagship product, HiNAS Control, delivers AI-based collision avoidance assistance and RPM optimization, significantly improving the safety and operational efficiency of maritime voyage.
HiNAS Control
Officer Of Watch’s Feedback
Go Farther, on the Same Fuel
•Automatic RPM optimization
Engine RPM is adjusted automatically to the optimized RPM plan, removing the burden of manual telegraph control by the Officer of the Watch (OOW).
•Efficient Voyage
The ship sails farther using same Fuel Oil Consumption (FOC) based on the calculated RPM by HiNAS Control.
•Sub-waypoint division
Routes are split more finely than conventional waypoint, giving RPM recommendations. As a result, HiNAS Control can save more fuel oil than other weather-routing services.
 
Prevent Accidents, Decide Sooner
•Collision Avoidance Assistance/Control (CAA/CAC)
This function watches nearby ships and checks for the risk of collision. If a risk of collision comes up, it makes a safe alternative route that follows the rules of the COLREGs and fits the traffic condition. After the risk of collision is gone, it can also guide the ship back to its original route.
•COG-based Track Control
Other ECDIS track control system use HDG, but HiNAS Control uses COG to keep the ship on its real track. HiNAS Control adjusts track control in real time for wind, waves, and currents, so the ship stays on track with less work for the OOW.
 
Consistent Control, Proven Reliability
•Fail-safe architecture
If HiNAS Control fails, connected equipment (AP, BMS, ECDIS) keeps operating normally.
•Immediate switching
Connected equipment (AP, BMS) can switch to manual instantly to respond emergency situation.
•Communication-loss resilience
CAA/CAC & Track Control function are maintained even if signal of V-SAT or Starlink is unstable.
•Verified performance
CAA/CAC & Track Control function are demonstrated and validated on vessels in active service — both newbuildings and retrofits — during sea trials with crew and class surveyor present.
FAQ

Q. Is HiNAS Control recognized by classification societies?

A. HiNAS Control holds Type Approval Cert from DNV, which was approved and issued on 7 April 2026. The system has been independently tested and verified, not just checked by its makers, giving shipowners & classification societies clear assurance that it’s safe and reliable at sea.

Q. Does it really save fuel on long voyages?

A. HiNAS Control helps reducing fuel consumption by approximately 5%, depending on vessel and operating conditions. It recommends the optimal engine RPM based on the voyage plan and ETA set by the captain, enabling more efficient voyage and longer distance with the same fuel consumption, without changing the route.

Q. We mostly sail in open sea, so when does collision avoidance help?

A. Our system supports safe ocean navigation by providing comprehensive functionality. HiNAS Control not only proposes collision avoidance control to avoid target vessels but also delivers alarm notifications when potentially hazardous vessels are detected. Additionally, it provides track control to maintain the navigation route planned by the captain throughout your voyage.

Q. What happens if the system fails or loses communication?

A. HiNAS Control is designed so that nothing is lost in the event of an issue. Even if HiNAS Control fails, the connected equipment (AP, BMS, ECDIS) continues to operate normally, and manual control can be switched instantly. In addition, its core functions, including Track Control and Collision Avoidance, remain available to support safe navigation.

Q. How do we get support after installation?
